Galápagos – Chinese Hat

加拉帕戈斯群岛 – 帽子岛


---------

<b>中国帽子岛</b>的火山锥实际上是由小型的火山口喷流出来的岩浆堆积而成。从北面看过去，帽子岛确实很像一顶帽子。不过这样的草帽不止在中国有，在越南和东南亚也很常见。

Viewed from the north, <b>Sombrero Chino</b> does look like a hat. But such a hat can be found not only in China, it is also very common in Vietnam and Southeast Asia.

-------


<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3665 w=840 h=640 float=center]Itinerary of Rábida island and Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Leaving Rábida island, the boat headed northeast along the coast of Santiago Island. In an hour or so, we arrived at <a href="https://www.galapagosislands.com/santiago/chinese-hat.html" data-type="URL" data-id="https://www.galapagosislands.com/santiago/chinese-hat.html">Sombrero Chino</a>, which means Chinese Hat in English. Such straw hats are also very common in Vietnam and Southeast Asia, with either round or pointed tops.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The volcanic cone of Sombrero Chino is called "<a href="https://geology.com/volcanoes/spatter-cones/" data-type="URL" data-id="https://geology.com/volcanoes/spatter-cones/">spatter cone</a>", which is actually the built-up of lava threw out by the volcano vent, so it is usually not too high. Originally part of Santiago Island, over time, weathering made the cone lower and lower, and the sea water gradually eroded the land connecting Santiago Island, creating a channel of about 600 feets.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3667 w=840 h=640 float=center]Looking from the north, Sombrero Chino does look like a hat[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We landed at the beach on the north side of the island. Wet landing is generally on the beach, where you disembark the boat in the water and walk to shore, while dry landing requires a suitable reef or dock so you can disembark directly onto land. Sombrero Chino is less than a quarter a square mile in size. We walked south along the coast facing the Santiago Island, with volcanic beaches all along the way. Low plants cover the ground in a variety of colors, from green to dark red, some even extremely bright red. The channel between the two islands is very narrow and the water is turquoise blue. </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3668 w=840 h=640 float=center]Volcanic beach of Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3678 w=840 h=640 float=center]Colorful Sea Purslane on the beach, Sea Purslane[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Various forms of volcanic rock can be seen on the island, both jagged 'a'a and gentler pahoehoe. Some are apparently pipes formed by the flow of cooling lava, and collapsed down later. It is similar to the craters on Santa Cruz Island, only on a much smaller scale.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3669 w=840 h=640 float=center]Collapsed volcanic tube, Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3670 w=840 h=640 float=center]Dead coral beach, Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There is a sea lion colony on Sombrero Chino. We saw many sea lions sunbathing on the beach, with the Sombrero Chino as the background.  The trail that you can walk on the island is only about 400 yards. At the end of the trail, we saw a few more familiar marine iguanas, crawling on the volcanic rocks and keeping themselves warm.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3671 w=840 h=640 float=center]A little sea line on the beach, Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3672 w=840 h=640 float=center]Marine iguanas on the rocks, Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Just as we were boarding our zodiac and preparing to return to the boat, we spotted two <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galapagos_penguin">Galápagos penguins</a> on the reef. We had seen them on our second day in the archipelago and had not seen them again until then. They are indeed small, nowhere near as imposing as the Emperor and King penguins. But they are very lucky not to have to go through the long, harsh winters that others do.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3674 w=840 h=640 float=center]Galápagos penguins, Sombrero Chino[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3675 w=840 h=640 float=center][/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The sea was calm, our boat was moored near Sombrero Chino for the night. As night fell, Marco took us to the top deck. There was no other light source around, so we could see the stars clearly. Marco used a small laser pointer in his hand to shoot a ray of light to point out the location of the constellations.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In the distant sky, we could faintly see a bright red light, in the direction of Isabela Island in the distance. Marco was particularly excited to see it, after confirming that it was indeed the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Negra_(Gal%C3%A1pagos)" data-type="URL" data-id="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Negra_(Gal%C3%A1pagos)">Sierra Negra</a> volcano on Isabela Island. We had walked at the foot of the volcano a few days before. This is the first time I saw the eruption of the volcano with my own eyes, but unfortunately it was too far away. I wanted to take a picture with my camera, but the boat was undulating so much, but you can get the sense of it in this picture.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3665 w=840 h=640 float=center]拉维达岛和帽子岛的行程[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>离开拉维达岛，游艇沿着圣地亚哥岛的海岸向东北方向行驶。一个小时左右，我们就到达我们下午的活动地点，中国帽子岛（Sombrero Chino）。这样的草帽在越南和东南亚也很常见，圆顶、尖顶的都有，姑且就叫它帽子岛吧。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>帽子岛的火山锥被称为“spatter cone”，实际上是小型的火山口喷流出来的岩浆堆积而成，所以一般都不会太高。最初帽子岛还是圣地亚哥岛的一部分，日积月累，风化和坍陷让山顶越来越低，海水逐渐侵蚀了连接圣地亚哥岛的陆地，形成了一道约200米的海峡。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3667 w=840 h=640 float=center]从北面看过去，帽子岛确实很像一顶帽子[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>我们在岛北面的沙滩湿登陆（wet landing）。所谓湿登陆一般是在沙滩上，下船的地方在水里，然后走到岸上；而干登陆（dry landing）要有合适的礁石或是码头停船，下船时直接登上陆地。帽子岛的面积只有不到半平方公里，我们沿着面向圣地亚哥岛一侧的海岸向南走，一路上都是火山岩海滩。<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E6%B5%B7%E9%A9%AC%E9%BD%BF/8298641" data-type="URL" data-id="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E6%B5%B7%E9%A9%AC%E9%BD%BF/8298641">海马齿</a>类的低矮植物覆地而生，五彩斑斓，从绿色到暗红，有些甚至是极为明亮的红色。两个岛之间的海峡非常窄，海水碧蓝。 </p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3668 w=840 h=640 float=center]帽子岛的火山岩沙滩[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3678 w=840 h=640 float=center]海滩上的五颜六色的海马齿类植物，帽子岛[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>岛上可以看到各种火山岩的形态，既有锯齿般的'a'a，也有较为平缓的pahoehoe。有的显然是岩浆流动冷却形成的管道，又塌陷下来，和圣克鲁兹岛上的深坑类似，只是规模小了很多。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3669 w=840 h=640 float=center]塌陷的岩浆管，帽子岛[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3670 w=840 h=640 float=center]铺满贝壳和死去的珊瑚的海滩，帽子岛[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>帽子岛上有一个海豹的种群，一路上看到不少海豹在沙滩上晒太阳，有的还不停地以帽子岛的圆顶做背景，搔首弄姿。岛上可以步行的小路只有大约400米。在小路的尽头，我们又看到几只熟悉的海蜥蜴，自顾自地爬在火山岩上 - 任你海浪滔天，我们抱团取暖。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3671 w=840 h=640 float=center]帽子岛上的小海狮[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3672 w=840 h=640 float=center]帽子岛上的海蜥蜴[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>就在我们登上皮筏艇，准备返回游艇的时候，在海中的礁石上发现了两只<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%8A%A0%E5%B2%9B%E7%8E%AF%E4%BC%81%E9%B9%85/9260835">加拉帕戈斯企鹅</a>。我们在来群岛的第二天就曾看到过它们的踪影，直到今天才又再见。它们的体型确实很小，远不如帝企鹅和王企鹅那么气宇轩昂。不过不需要像它们的同胞那样度过漫长严酷的冬天，应该算是非常幸运了吧。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3674 w=840 h=640 float=center]岸边礁石上的加拉帕戈斯企鹅，帽子岛[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3675 w=840 h=640 float=center]一同停泊在帽子岛附近的游船[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>因为这里风平浪静，我们的游艇就停泊在帽子岛的附近过夜。夜幕降临，Marco把我们带到三层的甲板上。海面上没有别的光源，可以清楚的看到满天繁星。Marco用手中小小的激光笔，就可以射出一道光柱，指点星座的位置。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>在远远的天边，能够隐隐约约看到红色的亮光，看方向是远处的伊莎贝拉岛。Marco见了也是特别的兴奋，经过确认，那确实是伊莎贝拉岛的黑山火山（Sierra Negra），我们前几天还在火山的脚下走过。这是我第一次亲眼看到火山的喷发，可惜离的实在太远。想用相机照下来，无奈船体在水面上起起伏伏，只能拍个大概。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
