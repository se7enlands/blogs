
Galápagos - Star Wars Beach

加拉帕戈斯群岛 - 星战海滩


<b>Puerto Egas, Santiago Island.</b> The erosion created many rock stacks along the shore, some of which are more than half a meter high. They have this layered swirling structure. The dark lava stack with the unique shape  covering large area as far as the eye can see. The view gives a rather supernatural feeling.

<b>圣地亚哥岛Puerto Egas海滩</b>的岩石因为海水的侵蚀形成漩涡状层层叠叠的结构，有的有半米多高。黝黑的火山岩加上放眼望去大片的独特地貌，给人一种颇为奇幻的感觉。


--------------


<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The sea off Isla Albany was calm and the sun was shining. The lunch was moved to the open space on the second deck.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3593 w=840 h=640 float=center]Lunch at Isla Albany, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The afternoon landing was at Puerto Egas, south of James Bay. It is the best place to see Galápagos Fur Seal. Fur Seal belongs to a subfamily within the sea lion family. They are smaller than sea lions and have a slightly shorter muzzle. It's really not easy to tell them apart without looking closely. On the rocks along the shore stand a few Yellow-crowned Night-herons, the sea breeze blowing their plumes slightly. The night-herons rarely move, standing alone in the wind is the main thing they do throughout the day.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3626 w=840 h=640 float=center]Galápagos Fur Seal, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3622 w=840 h=640 float=center]Yellow-crowned Night-heron, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Marco led us walking along the coastline. This section of the coast is formed by volcanic rocks. The sea shore zigzags and the terrain is undulating. Repeated slapping of the waves formed the tide pools in different sizes among the rocks. There is a stone bridge where the waves are strong and  the erosion is the most severe. This is one of the unique landmark of Puerto Egas, and everyone took pictures here.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3617 w=840 h=640 float=center]Stone bridge, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The erosion created many rock stacks along the shore, some of which are more than half a meter high. They have this layered swirling structure. The dark lava stack with the unique shape  covering large area as far as the eye can see. The view gives a rather supernatural feeling.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3621 w=840 h=640 float=center]Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3619 w=840 h=640 float=center]Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Marine iguanas and red crabs (Sally Lightfoot crab) were found everywhere. The red stone crab is not endemic to the Galápagos, but can be found on tropical and subtropical seashores throughout the Americas. They are agile and adept climbers, often clinging to the lava rocks. The immature crabs are not as brightly colored, and the night herons and rock herons that stand quietly on the shore are their major natural predators.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3618 w=840 h=640 float=center]Marine Iguana, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3623 w=840 h=640 float=center]Sally Lightfoot Crab, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3625 w=840 h=640 float=center]Sally Lightfoot Crab, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The beach without volcanic rocks is often covered with a type of  vines called beach morning glory. It is also called saddle vine because the opposite leaves along the vine resemble a saddle. Their pink trumpet-shaped flowers are unbelievably beautiful on the barren beach, but more importantly, beach morning glory is saline-tolerant and can prevent sand erosion. It is also used as a herb in China.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3620 w=840 h=640 float=center]Beach Morning Glory, Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We walked to the southern point of James Bay. The black beach is made by the eroded lava rocks. The rock formation at the end of the beach looks like the All Terrain Walkers (AT-AT) from Star Wars. The mysterious landscape leads us to an alien planet.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3627 w=840 h=640 float=center]Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>We boarded the ship here. From the ship we could see the abandoned buildings on the shore. This was built for the salt mines back in the 1920s.  It is said that there is also an open space nearby, which was a soccer field in those days.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3614 w=840 h=640 float=center]Abandoned constructions at Puerto Egas, Santiago Island[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Our boat traveled south along the coastline and woud arrive in the evening at Rábida, a small island to the south of Santiago Island.</p>

<p>Isla Albany附近的海面平静，阳光明媚。船上的午餐移到了二层的甲板上。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3593 w=840 h=640 float=center]在Isla Albany午餐，圣地亚哥岛[/singlepic]

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>下午的登陆点是位于James Bay南部的Puerto Egas。这里最容易看到加拉帕戈斯<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E6%B5%B7%E7%8B%97/82537" data-type="URL" data-id="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E6%B5%B7%E7%8B%97/82537">海狗</a>（Galápagos Fur Seal）的地方。 海狗属于海狮科里的一个亚科，体型比海狮小，吻部稍短，不仔细看确实不容易分辨。岸边的岩石上静静地站着几支<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E9%BB%84%E5%86%A0%E5%A4%9C%E9%B9%AD%E5%8A%A0%E6%8B%89%E5%B8%95%E6%88%88%E6%96%AF%E4%BA%9A%E7%A7%8D/14077608" data-type="URL" data-id="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E9%BB%84%E5%86%A0%E5%A4%9C%E9%B9%AD%E5%8A%A0%E6%8B%89%E5%B8%95%E6%88%88%E6%96%AF%E4%BA%9A%E7%A7%8D/14077608">黄冠夜鹭</a>（Yellow-crowned Night-heron），海风吹着它们头上的翎羽微微摇曳，夜鹭却一动不动， 在风中独立就是它们一天中主要做的事情。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Marco带领我们沿着海岸线前行。这是一片火山岩石形成的海岸线，犬牙交错、起伏不平。因为海浪的反复冲刷，岩石之间形成了大大小小的潮池（Tide Pool）。冲刷严重的地方更是形成了一座石桥。这是Puerto Egas很有特色的一个地貌，每个人都在这里照相留念。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3617 w=840 h=640 float=center]石桥，Puerto Egas，圣地亚哥岛[/singlepic]
<!-- /wp:shortcode -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>海水的腐蚀让海边的岩石形成漩涡状层层叠叠的结构，有的有半米多高。黝黑的火山岩加上放眼望去大片的独特地貌，给人一种颇为奇幻的感觉。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:shortcode -->
[singlepic id=3621 w=840 h=640 float=center]Puerto Egas，圣地亚哥岛[/singlepic]
[singlepic id=3619 w=840 h=640 float=center]Puerto Egas，圣地亚哥岛[/singlepic]

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>海鬣蜥和<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E7%BA%A2%E7%9F%B3%E8%9F%B9/4777314" data-type="URL" data-id="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E7%BA%A2%E7%9F%B3%E8%9F%B9/4777314">红石蟹</a>（Sally Lightfoot crab）随处可见。红石蟹并不是加拉帕戈斯的独特物种，在美洲的热带和亚热带的海边都可以见到。它们动作敏捷，擅于攀爬，经常附着在垂直、甚至向外倾斜的岩石上。未成年的石蟹颜色并没有那么鲜艳，静静站在岸边的夜鹭、岩鹭就是石蟹最主要的天敌之一。</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<p>没有火山岩的滩涂上长满了<a href="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%8E%9A%E8%97%A4/8298659" data-type="URL" data-id="https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%8E%9A%E8%97%A4/8298659">厚藤</a>（Beach Morning Glory）。因为对生的叶子状似马鞍，又叫马鞍藤。它开出的花呈粉红色的喇叭形，是一种耐盐碱的藤类植物，多生长在海滩上，能起到防止沙土流失的作用，还是一味中草药。</p>

<!-- wp:shortcode -->

<p>向北走到James Bay的最下沿，这是一片黑色的沙滩，沙滩尽头的岩石貌似星球大战中的All Terrain Walkers（AT-AT），一路奇幻的地貌似乎引领我们走入了外星球。</p>
